Tower of Salvos

What is it?

Tower of Salvos is a game made with Unreal Editor for Fortnite (UEFN) for the UNCG UEFN Game Jam 2023. It’s a multiplayer mini-game where players are tasked with racing to the top of a tower while outrunning the dangerous rising water. Depending on the order in which players reach the top, they will be rewarded with varying levels of equipment for the final battle. At the jam, the game was given one of three “Most Complete Game” awards and the “Best Gameplay Overall” award.

How?

The game was made in under 48 hours with a team of three and our team met for the first time on the day of the event. The game jam was primarily an educational endeavor facilitated by Epic Games, with educators from Cleverlike Studios helping everyone learn how to use Unreal Editor for Fortnite (UEFN). UEFN is exactly what it sounds like; it’s a version of Unreal Engine that is designed to work with Fortnite. While there are many limitations and unique quirks to UEFN, it still fundamentally works like Unreal Engine. Though none of us had experience with UEFN, we all shared a good amount of experience in game development in Unreal Engine and other engines, so we were able to pick it up fairly easily with the help of the teachers there.

We came up with the idea and basic design of the game by combining a few different ideas we had. While working with Fortnite had a lot of limitations, we wanted to take advantage of all it had to offer. So, we aimed to make a multiplayer game that used many of Fortnite’s already existing systems. My role on the team was implementing most of the technical mechanics. While many functions we needed already existed in Fortnite, there were plenty that we needed to make ourselves such as teleporting players correctly, granting items and implementing the rising water. I also designed the tower and how players navigate it.

About a month after the jam, our team found some more time to polish the game before publishing it to Fortnite Creative. Since we weren’t under time pressure, we were able to fix many bugs and refine the flow of gameplay.

Status

The game is considered finished and we are currently in the process of publishing the project to Fortnite. Until then, I have created a showcase video (linked below) that goes over the game and all its mechanics.
